FREQUENTLY ASK QUESTIONS

How much does it cost to hire a lawyer?

The cost depends on the type of case, the lawyer’s experience, and the time required. Some lawyers charge hourly rates, while others offer flat fees or work on a contingency basis. It’s important to discuss fees upfront.

How long will my case take?

The duration of your case depends on multiple factors, including the type of case, its complexity, the legal procedures involved, and how quickly all parties respond to filings or requests. For example, straightforward cases like drafting a legal document or negotiating a basic agreement might take just a few weeks, while complex matters such as litigation, divorces, or business disputes could take months or even years. Additionally, court schedules, the availability of witnesses, and the need for additional evidence or expert opinions can impact timelines. It’s also worth noting that some cases face delays due to court backlogs or unexpected complications.

While I’ll provide you with an estimated timeline based on my experience with similar cases, I’ll make sure to keep you updated at every stage and work to resolve your matter as efficiently as possible.

How strong is my case?

The strength of your case depends on the specific facts, the evidence available, and how the law applies to your situation. I will need to carefully review all documents, witness statements, and other details to assess its merits. It’s important to remember that even strong cases can face challenges, such as procedural issues, opposing arguments, or unforeseen evidence from the other side. I’ll provide you with an honest assessment and discuss any weaknesses or risks so we can work on building the best possible strategy for your case.

What does the initial consultation include?

The initial consultation is an opportunity for us to discuss the basics of your case. I’ll ask questions to understand your situation, identify key legal issues, and determine how I might be able to assist you. It’s also a chance for you to ask me questions about my experience, fees, and the legal process. While we may not delve into full legal strategies at this stage, I’ll provide you with an overview of your options and the next steps. This meeting helps us decide whether we’re the right fit to work together.
